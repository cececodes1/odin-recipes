N'awlins Muffuletta

The Muffuletta, the classic New Orleans sandwhich, is suprisingly easy to make at home. This Muffuletta recipe-complete with flavorful cold cuts, sliced cheese, and homemade olive spead, authenticalluy delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound loaves Italian bread
  • 8 ounces thinly sliced Genoa salami
  • 8 ounces thinly sliced cooked ham
  • 8 ounces sliced mortadella
  • 8 ounces sliced mozzarella cheese
  • 8 ounces provolone cheese
  • 1 cup pimento-stuffed green oilves, crushed
  • 1/2 cup drained kalamata olives, crushed
  • 1/2 cup pepperoncini, drained
  • 1/4 cup roughly chopped pickled cauliflower florets
  • 1/4 cup marinated cocktail onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons drained capers
  • 1 tablespoon chopped celery
  • 1 tablespoon chopped carrot
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon celery seed
  • 1/2 cup olive oil
  • 1/4 cup canola oil
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar

Steps

  1. To make the olive salad: Roughly chop together green olives, kalamata olives, pepperoncini, cauliflower, cocktail onions, garlic, capers, celery, and carrot.
  2. Combine with oregani, basil, black pepper, and celery seed in a medium bowl. Add in olive oil, canola oil, and vinegar. Mix together and transfer the mixture into a glass jar. Pour in more oil to civer if needed.
  3. Cover jar and refridgerate at least 8 hours to overnight.
  4. Cut loaves of bread in half horizontally, hollow out some of the excess bread to make room for filling.
  5. Spread each pieve if bread with equal amounts of the olive salad, including oil
  6. Layer bottom half of each with 1/2 of the salami, hame, mortadella, mozzarella, and provolone slices
  7. Replace top half on each loaf and cut the sandwhiches into equal parts
  8. Serve immediatley, or wrap tightly and refridgerate for a few hours; this will allow for the flavors to mingle and the live salad to soak into the bread.
